Alan Turing

Father of modern computing

Germany's Enigma

The Enigma machine was a major part in Nazi Germany's secret communications during World War II and used a complex encryption to send messages. It was a challenge to decrypt because the encryption key changed frequently.

Bombe

Multiple allied countries attempted to break code, but most were unsuccessful. Turing worked at Britain's Bletchley Park in secret with a team of researchers and they constructed a machine called the Bombe. The Allies the were able to acquire an intact Enigma unit, which Turing and his team used to assist in deciphering the machine using Bombe.

Legacy

Once deciphered, the Allies were able to intercept many encrypted German orders in secret. This helped the Allies gain a major advantage and saved many lives. While the Allies kept his work secret for many years, it was later made public and has had a great impact on modern technology.
